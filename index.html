How Malaysia Moves

Malaysia’s public transport network reveals how rail infrastructure, ridership recovery, and changing travel patterns shape everyday mobility across the country.

01 · NETWORK

Malaysia Railway Network

Malaysia’s rail network is unevenly distributed, with stronger access along major urban and west coast corridors.

This map shows where rail infrastructure is located, with stations mainly concentrated along Peninsular Malaysia’s west coast corridor.

02 · RECOVERY

Ridership Recovery and Growth

Ridership fell sharply during the pandemic period, but most services show a gradual recovery after 2022.

Daily Ridership Trend by Rail Service

The line chart shows daily rail ridership over time, highlighting the sharp fall during disruption periods and the gradual recovery after 2022.

Bus and Rail Ridership Composition

This proportional bar chart compares bus and rail shares by year, showing that rail consistently contributes the larger share of ridership.

Rail Service Ridership Distribution

The box plot summarises ridership spread by service, showing that LRT KJ and MRT Kajang have higher typical ridership than Monorail.

03 · PATTERNS

Daily and Monthly Travel Patterns

Monthly patterns show that public transport demand is not constant, with stronger recovery visible in recent years.

MRT Kajang Ridership Heatmap

The heatmap compares monthly MRT Kajang ridership across years, with darker months after 2023 showing stronger recovery in demand.

Bus and Rail Mode Share

Rail contributes the larger overall share, while bus remains an important supporting mode in daily mobility.

Average Rail Service Ranking

The ranking chart compares average rail ridership by service, showing LRT Kelana Jaya as the busiest and Monorail as the lowest.

04 · EXPLORE

Interactive Exploration

Interactive views allow users to explore individual services and compare relationships between different transport modes.

Interactive Transport Mode Explorer

This interactive chart lets users select one service and observe how its ridership changed before, during, and after the recovery period.

Bus and Rail Ridership Relationship

The scatter plot compares daily bus and rail demand after 2022, showing that higher bus ridership usually appears with higher rail ridership.

Yearly Bus and Rail Gap

The connected dot plot compares yearly bus and rail averages, showing that the gap widened after 2023 and then became more stable.

💡

Key Insight

Malaysia’s public transport data shows a clear recovery story after major disruption periods. Rail services dominate overall ridership, while bus services still play an important supporting role in the wider mobility system. The main challenge is not only recovery, but also building a more balanced and accessible transport network across different regions.
